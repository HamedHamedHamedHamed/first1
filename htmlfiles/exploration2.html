PetroDara continues to re-interpret 3D seismic data incorporating the new well data obtained from development drilling for better improvement the subsurface structures image.

Seismic Activity

The company continues to re-interpret the 3D seismic data using the new acquired data from the new drilled wells in order to get the best picking of faults and geological structures, PetroDara takes the strategy of continuous updating of the data through the bore hole seismic acquisition, which is considered one of the most important tools for standardization and improvement for the original seismic data.



Exploration and Development Activities

No exploratory wells were drilled during the fiscal year (2017/2018) but two wells were drilled (Arta-48 and Arta-54) in Arta Development Lease with total footage reached up to 8880 feet (2707 m.) with success ratio of 100%.

Arta-48

The Development well Arta-48 was spud on January 1st, 2018 in Arta Development Lease, to produce oil from Sandy Limestone and Sandstone of Nukhul Formation of Miocene age. The well reached final total depth of 4120 feet (1256 m.) on January 10th, 2018 within the Limestone of Thebes Formation of Eocene age. Good gas and oil shows were reported while drilling in the Sandstones of Lower Nukhul Formation. Wireline electric logs have been confirmed presence of an encouraging hydrocarbon accumulation zone to be tested in the Sandstones of Lower Nukhul Formation. A total of 37 feet was perforated in the intervals (3770 - 3807). The well was put on production with an initial rate of 240 BOPD.

Arta-54

The development well Arta-54 was spud on May 4rd, 2018 in Arta Development Lease, to produce oil from Sandy Limestones and Sandstones of Nukhul Formation of Miocene age. The well reached final total depth of 4760 feet (1451m.) on May 13th, 2018 within the Limestone of Thebes Formation of Eocene age. Good gas and oil shows were reported while drilling in the Sandstones of Lower Nukhul Formation. Wireline electric logs have been confirmed presence of an encouraging hydrocarbon accumulation zone to be tested in the Sandstones of Lower Nukhul Formation. A total of 23 feet was perforated in the intervals (3404 - 3418) & (3423 - 3432). The well was put on production with an initial rate of 80 BOPD.
